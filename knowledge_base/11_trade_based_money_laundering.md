# Trade-Based Money Laundering

## Overview

Trade-based money laundering uses trade transactions to move value and disguise illicit funds through mispriced invoices, false descriptions, phantom shipments, or complex trade routes.

## Why It Matters

Trade transactions can provide a legitimate-looking explanation for cross-border payments, while criminals exploit documentation complexity and international supply chains.

## Typical Indicators

- Invoice value inconsistent with market price.
- Goods description is vague or inconsistent.
- Payment route does not match shipment route.
- Repeated trade payments with limited documentation.
- Use of shell companies or recently created entities.

## Analyst Considerations

Review invoices, contracts, shipping documents, goods description, counterparty profile, and whether payment value aligns with trade activity.

## Example Scenario

A company sends repeated high-value payments for generic goods to a newly formed overseas entity with no clear trade history.

## Suggested Action

Escalate where trade documentation is missing, inconsistent, or does not explain value and route of funds.
