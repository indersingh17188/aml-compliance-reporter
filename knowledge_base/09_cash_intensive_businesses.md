# Cash-Intensive Businesses

## Overview

Cash-intensive businesses receive a significant portion of revenue in cash, such as restaurants, convenience stores, bars, salons, or small retail shops.

## Why It Matters

Cash activity can be hard to verify. Illicit funds may be mixed with real business revenue, making the money appear legitimate.

## Typical Indicators

- Cash deposits inconsistent with business size or seasonality.
- Repeated deposits just below thresholds.
- Revenue patterns inconsistent with similar businesses.
- Rapid transfer of deposited funds to unrelated accounts.
- Limited card payments despite high reported revenue.

## Analyst Considerations

Compare activity with expected business profile, location, seasonality, revenue model, and historical patterns. Review support documents when available.

## Example Scenario

A small restaurant deposits unusually large amounts of cash and transfers funds overseas within days.

## Suggested Action

Escalate when cash activity is inconsistent, repetitive, structured, or followed by rapid onward movement.
