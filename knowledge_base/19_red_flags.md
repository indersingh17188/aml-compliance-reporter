# AML Red Flags

## Overview

AML red flags are indicators that a transaction, customer, or pattern may require closer review. A red flag is not proof of money laundering.

## Why It Matters

Red flags help analysts prioritize review. Several moderate indicators together may be more meaningful than one isolated signal.

## Typical Indicators

- Activity inconsistent with customer profile.
- Multiple transactions with similar values.
- Rapid movement of funds after receipt.
- High-value transaction with unclear purpose.
- Cross-border transfer with no obvious rationale.
- Cross-currency movement with amount discrepancy.
- Unusual timing such as night or burst activity.

## Analyst Considerations

Assess red flags in combination. Consider customer profile, historical activity, amount, timing, geography, counterparties, and supporting documentation.

## Example Scenario

A customer sends a high-value cross-currency transfer at night to an unrelated recipient and has limited prior activity.

## Suggested Action

Escalate when multiple red flags appear together or activity cannot be explained by the customer profile.
