# Politically Exposed Person Transactions

## Overview

A Politically Exposed Person, or PEP, is someone who holds or has held a prominent public function, or is closely associated with such a person. PEP status does not imply wrongdoing.

## Why It Matters

PEP transactions can require enhanced due diligence because of corruption, bribery, and misuse of public funds risk. The key question is legitimacy of source of funds and wealth.

## Typical Indicators

- High-value transfers inconsistent with income.
- Payments from government contractors or state-owned entities.
- Use of family members, associates, or companies as intermediaries.
- Unclear source of funds or wealth.
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions.

## Analyst Considerations

Review PEP status, role, jurisdiction, source of wealth, source of funds, beneficial ownership, adverse media, and transaction purpose.

## Example Scenario

A public official’s associate receives large payments from a company with recent government contracts and quickly transfers funds abroad.

## Suggested Action

Apply enhanced due diligence and escalate if source of funds, purpose, or counterparty relationship is unclear.
