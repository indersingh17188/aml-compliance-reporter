# Large Value Transactions

## Overview

Large value transactions are not automatically suspicious, but require contextual review. The key question is whether the amount is consistent with customer profile and source of funds.

## Why It Matters

Large transfers can represent placement, layering, or integration of illicit funds. High value increases potential impact and may trigger internal review requirements.

## Typical Indicators

- Amount inconsistent with customer income or business size.
- Sudden high-value transfer after low historical activity.
- Large transfer to unknown or unrelated counterparty.
- High value combined with currency conversion or cross-border movement.
- Lack of supporting documentation.

## Analyst Considerations

Review source of funds, source of wealth, customer history, occupation or business activity, purpose of payment, and counterparty relationship.

## Example Scenario

An individual with modest declared income sends a large international transfer to a company with no obvious relationship.

## Suggested Action

Request documentation or escalate when amount is inconsistent with profile or transaction purpose is unclear.
