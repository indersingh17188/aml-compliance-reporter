# Enhanced Due Diligence

## Overview

Enhanced Due Diligence, or EDD, is deeper review applied when a customer, transaction, or relationship presents elevated AML risk.

## Why It Matters

EDD helps determine whether activity is legitimate, suspicious, or requires escalation. It is relevant for high-risk customers, PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, unusual transactions, and complex ownership.

## Typical Indicators

- Need to verify source of funds.
- Need to verify source of wealth.
- Transaction inconsistent with customer profile.
- Purpose or counterparty relationship unclear.
- Sanctions, PEP, or adverse media checks may be needed.

## Analyst Considerations

EDD should be proportionate. A single medium-risk factor may not require escalation, but combined factors such as high amount, cross-border transfer, and unclear purpose may justify deeper review.

## Example Scenario

A high-value cross-border transfer is made by a customer with no international business activity and unclear counterparty relationship.

## Suggested Action

Request supporting documentation and escalate where the explanation is missing, inconsistent, or unsupported.
