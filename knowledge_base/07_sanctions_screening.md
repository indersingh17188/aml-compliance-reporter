# Sanctions Screening

## Overview

Sanctions screening checks whether customers, counterparties, entities, owners, vessels, or jurisdictions are subject to restrictions. Sanctions risk is distinct from general AML risk.

## Why It Matters

A sanctions match can create legal and regulatory exposure. Even without a direct match, activity may suggest evasion through intermediaries, shell companies, or unusual routes.

## Typical Indicators

- Counterparty name resembles a sanctioned party.
- Transaction involves restricted country, sector, or entity.
- Intermediaries obscure final beneficiary.
- Sudden change in payment route or bank.
- Vague descriptions or missing originator/beneficiary information.

## Analyst Considerations

Potential sanctions matches should follow internal sanctions procedures. Do not clear them based only on model output. Review screening results, ownership, geography, and transaction context.

## Example Scenario

A payment is routed through multiple entities with a beneficiary name similar to a restricted entity and limited payment description.

## Suggested Action

Follow sanctions escalation procedures immediately for potential true matches. Do not treat sanctions issues as ordinary AML alerts.
