# Beneficial Ownership

## Overview

Beneficial ownership identifies the natural person or persons who ultimately own or control a customer or entity. Complex ownership structures can hide real control.

## Why It Matters

Unknown or opaque ownership increases AML risk because criminals may use companies, nominees, trusts, or intermediaries to disguise control.

## Typical Indicators

- Complex ownership structure without clear reason.
- Nominee directors or shareholders.
- Missing or inconsistent ownership information.
- Entities registered in secrecy jurisdictions.
- Transactions benefit parties not visible in records.

## Analyst Considerations

Review ownership documents, control rights, directors, signatories, related entities, and whether a PEP or sanctioned party may be connected.

## Example Scenario

A company receives high-value transfers, but ownership records show several layers of foreign entities and no clear natural person owner.

## Suggested Action

Apply enhanced due diligence when ownership is unclear, inconsistent, or connected to high-risk jurisdictions, PEPs, or shell-company indicators.
