# Shell Companies

## Overview

A shell company is a legal entity with little or no active business operations. Some are legitimate, but they can be used to hide ownership, move funds, or disguise origin and destination.

## Why It Matters

Shell companies can create distance between illicit funds and the people controlling them, and may support layering, integration, sanctions evasion, or corruption schemes.

## Typical Indicators

- Unclear or generic business purpose.
- Shared registered address used by many entities.
- Nominee directors or owners.
- Transaction volume inconsistent with declared activity.
- Consulting or service fees with limited detail.

## Analyst Considerations

Review beneficial ownership, business activity, transaction rationale, invoices, contracts, and entity relationships. Consider whether there is economic substance.

## Example Scenario

A newly formed company receives large payments from multiple jurisdictions but has no visible operations, staff, website, or clear business purpose.

## Suggested Action

Escalate where shell company indicators combine with high-value movement, cross-border transfer, sanctions risk, or unclear ownership.
