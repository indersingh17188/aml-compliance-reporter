# Analyst Reporting Guidelines

## Overview

AML analyst reports should explain why a transaction was reviewed, which risk indicators were observed, what evidence was considered, and what action is recommended.

## Why It Matters

Clear documentation supports auditability, internal review, and compliance decision-making. A good report separates model findings, facts, retrieved guidance, and recommendation.

## Typical Indicators

- Risk score and risk level are documented.
- Key transaction facts are summarized.
- Model explanation or SHAP drivers are listed.
- Relevant AML typology or guidance is referenced.
- Recommended action is clear.

## Analyst Considerations

Avoid unsupported conclusions. Use cautious language such as may indicate, is consistent with, or requires review. Separate automated model output from analyst judgement.

## Example Scenario

The transaction was classified high risk by the model. Main risk drivers include cross-currency movement, high-value transfer, and unusual timing. These indicators may be consistent with layering and require review.

## Suggested Action

For high-risk alerts, recommend escalation, supporting documentation review, source of funds verification, and counterparty relationship assessment.
