# High-Risk Jurisdictions

## Overview

High-risk jurisdictions are countries or territories associated with elevated money laundering, terrorist financing, corruption, secrecy, sanctions, or weak controls.

## Why It Matters

Transactions involving higher-risk jurisdictions may require deeper review because customer identity, source of funds, ownership, and counterparty purpose can be harder to verify.

## Typical Indicators

- Transfers to or from jurisdictions rated high risk internally.
- Activity inconsistent with known geographic footprint.
- Frequent movement through offshore or secrecy jurisdictions.
- Cross-border transfers without clear rationale.
- Use of intermediaries or complex routes.

## Analyst Considerations

Assess whether the customer normally operates in the jurisdiction. Review purpose, counterparty relationship, source of funds, sanctions and adverse media where needed.

## Example Scenario

A local-only customer sends repeated payments to entities in jurisdictions unrelated to their occupation, business, or personal profile.

## Suggested Action

Enhanced due diligence is recommended when jurisdiction risk appears with large value, cross-currency movement, shell companies, or unknown counterparties.
