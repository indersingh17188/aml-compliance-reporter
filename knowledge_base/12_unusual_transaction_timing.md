# Unusual Transaction Timing

## Overview

Unusual timing refers to transactions occurring at times that do not match expected behaviour, such as late-night transfers, weekend bursts, rapid sequences, or activity immediately after funds are received.

## Why It Matters

Timing alone is rarely enough to classify a transaction as suspicious, but it strengthens risk when combined with high value, cross-border movement, multiple accounts, or unusual payment formats.

## Typical Indicators

- Late-night or early-morning transactions.
- Multiple transactions in a short period.
- Weekend or holiday activity inconsistent with profile.
- Funds transferred onward immediately after receipt.
- Sudden burst of activity after inactivity.

## Analyst Considerations

Compare timing with historical behaviour, occupation, geography, and transaction purpose. Interpret timing together with amount, counterparty, currency, and account activity.

## Example Scenario

A customer who normally transacts during business hours sends multiple high-value transfers between 01:00 and 04:00.

## Suggested Action

Escalate if unusual timing appears with high-risk SHAP drivers such as cross-bank movement, amount discrepancy, or cross-currency transfer.
