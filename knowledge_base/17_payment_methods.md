# Payment Methods and Formats

## Overview

Payment method describes the channel or format used to move funds, such as wire transfer, ACH, cheque, card, or cash. Different formats carry different speed, traceability, and risk.

## Why It Matters

Some formats may be associated with rapid movement, cross-border activity, or reduced transparency. Risk depends on customer profile and transaction context.

## Typical Indicators

- Sudden change in preferred payment method.
- High-value wire transfer inconsistent with prior activity.
- Cheques or ACH used repeatedly in structured patterns.
- Payment format combined with unusual time, amount, or counterparty.
- Rapid movement after receipt by the same or different format.

## Analyst Considerations

Compare payment format with historical behaviour. Review whether format is appropriate for transaction purpose, amount, and counterparty.

## Example Scenario

A customer who normally uses small domestic ACH payments suddenly initiates a large international wire transfer to an unrelated company.

## Suggested Action

Escalate when payment format changes combine with high value, cross-border transfer, unclear purpose, or unusual timing.
