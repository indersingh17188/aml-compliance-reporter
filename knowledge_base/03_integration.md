# Integration

## Overview

Integration is the stage where illicit funds are reintroduced into the legitimate economy through business revenue, asset purchases, investments, loan repayments, or similar activity.

## Why It Matters

Integrated funds can be difficult to distinguish from legitimate wealth. Suspicion usually comes from inconsistency between customer profile and value, frequency, or purpose.

## Typical Indicators

- High-value purchases inconsistent with known income.
- Sudden increase in account activity.
- Business revenue not matching business profile.
- Use of companies with unclear ownership.
- Payments labelled as loans or consulting fees without support.

## Analyst Considerations

Assess whether activity matches occupation, business model, income, source of wealth, and source of funds. Review supporting documentation and ownership information.

## Example Scenario

A small business suddenly receives large payments labelled as consulting income from unrelated foreign entities.

## Suggested Action

Request supporting documentation and consider enhanced due diligence where economic rationale is unclear.
